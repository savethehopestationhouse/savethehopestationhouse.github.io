Save the Hope Station House

The Hope Station House, built in 1916, is an integral part of the history of Hope, BC. Tragically, we are at serious risk of losing this important part of our heritage. The District of Hope's Council plans on putting out an RFP for quotes to demolish this historic building before January 22, 2021. While time is running out, it's still not too late to do something.

Please read on to learn how you can join us in raising your voice to save this important part of Hope's and the Fraser Valley's history!

Investing in our Future

Investing in the preservation, rehabilitation and ongoing use of heritage buildings such as the Hope Station House has a proven track record of creating economic benefits for BC communities. Towns such as Nelson, Fort Langley, and Agassiz each boasting historic train stations as a central feature of their downtown core, have a thriving tourism industry based on their preserved history.

Heritage conservation enriches the urban fabric of a town and attracts tourists and locals alike, generating jobs and leading to economic growth.

How did we get Here?

The District of Hope's Council passed a motion to destroy this century-old building in a settlement of $650,000 with the Ministry of Transportation and Infrastructure ( Source ). According to Mayor Peter Robb in an article published in the Hope Standard, the Ministry of Transportation failed to undertake consultation with First Nations. Because of their error, the Ministry of Transportation was forced to rescind their offer of sale to the District of Hope, of the land upon which the Hope Station House sits. Instead they entered into negotiations with Hope and negotiated a settlement of $650,000. The District of Hope, however, is not being forced by the Ministry of Transportation and Infrastructure to destroy the Hope Station. As our elected representatives, if it is our will that the Hope Station be saved from demolition, it is at their discretion to pay heed.
